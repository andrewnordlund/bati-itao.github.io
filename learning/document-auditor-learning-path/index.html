Learning Path for Document Accessibility Auditors

Accessibility Auditor

With this learning path, you will have a basic understanding of the technologies that the Web is built on, a basic understanding of accessibility as it pertains to the Web, mobile, documents, and eventually desktop; and how to build and evaluate rich internet applications. No course can be exhaustive. Most knowledge will be gained through experience. This learning path will lay the foundation allowing you to build upon it as you practice web development and accessibility auditing.

Note: the courses provided by the Government of Canada are available in both Official Languages. The rest, however, are mostly English only.

Block E. Advance learning on Accessible Microsoft and PDF Documents

Objective: To prepare for the Accessible Document Specialist (ADS) Exam in order to obtain the ADS Certification from the International Association of Accessibility of Professionals (IAAP).

Prerequisite: You must have an excellent knowledge about WCAG 2.2-Understanding the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. You mush have hands on experience with documents audits and remediations of a minimum 1-2 years' experience who function in one of many team roles related to accessible document authoring, remediation, training, policy, and advocacy of accessible document solutions (extract from Accessible Document Specialist-IAAP). The approval from your team leader and/or manager is required.
